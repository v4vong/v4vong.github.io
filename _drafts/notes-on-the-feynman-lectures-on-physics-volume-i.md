## 1. Atoms in Motion

### What is the atomic hypothesis?

All things are made of atoms—little particles that move around in perpetual motion, attracting each other when they are a little distance apart, but repelling upon each other when they are squeezed.

### What phenomenon can be explained by the atomic hypothesis?

- Liquid and solid substances retain their volume. Due to the attraction of atoms, they don't expand, and due to the repelling of atoms, they don't collapse.
- We can explain heat as the motion of atoms. If we increase the temperature, we increase the motion of atoms. The accelerated motion of atoms will break the original balance formed by the attraction and repulsion between atoms, so this increases the volume of matter. If the atoms are moving so fast that attraction can't hold them together, some of the atoms will fly off; this is the process of evaporation.
- The pressure of the gas on an object is formed by a large number of atoms hitting the object. So it's not hard to imagine that if we increase the density of the gas, we increase the pressure. Besides, if we increase the temperature, we increase the motion of atoms, so it also increase the pressure.
- A chemical reaction can be explained as the process in which the rearrangement of the atomic partners occurs.
